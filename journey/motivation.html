1.Japan Motivation

Motivation is a funny thing. You never really know where it will come from. I learned this with my trip to Japan, which all started with an insult. One that would motivate me to travel around the world all to prove a point.

It was a pretty normal day just chatting with friends in a group chat. That is until two of them broke some big news. They had planned a trip to Japan. As any good friends would do, another friend of mine Luis and I sized the opportunity for some lite hearted joking, and fake hurt and disappoint over the fact our so called friends did not invite us. I don't think either of us was serious about being left out. To be honest Japan was never on my list of must visit places at the time, but then! Boom! Everything changed with one message...one sentence...one second, motivation swarmed over me in that instance. Japan jumped to the top place I was going to visit. It read, "Y’all could not afford it with how much y’all make so we didn't invite you". At that moment Luis must have had the same idea because I got a private message from him simply stating, "So are we doing this!"

From that point on Luis and I did what any normal person would do. We faked interest in their trip to learn key details about it. Questions like, when would they be arriving? How long are they staying? What cities are you visiting? The plan was simple, try to show up everywhere they do in Japan. Unfortunately, we later learned they had just booked a cruise around Japan. However, Luis and I was already invested. Seeing as we had just bought plan tickets that happened to arrive in Japan the same day they did. We decided even if we can't pop up everywhere, they are at, at the very least we can surprise them when they arrived. With the right motivation, anything is possible.
